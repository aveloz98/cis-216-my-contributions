Navigating College Life After Becoming A Young Parent

Being a parent has proven to be an unknowing & hard lifelong milestone for both parent and child.

So how do you navigate jumping from being a child straight into becoming a parent? Everyone knows when you become a college student it will be a time
of failing and finding yourself. College life is universally known to be a time to fail and learn unaided to discover motivation to better yourself. In short,
independence is key to focusing on yourself but how do you achieve this if you are too focused on keeping another person fed, changed, healthy, happy, and loved?
While it is easier said than done a balance must be found to pride yourself both as a person and a parent.

Components of Finding a Balance:

  1. Let go of the belief your personality is just a parent
  2. Rewarding yourself with any small achievement made as a person or parent
  3. Allowing yourself time for going out with friends every once in a while

Communities Everywhere Encourage These Morals

Whether someone is seeking an online community or in-person interaction there is an endless amount of support resources everywhere. Anyone can search for local
groups on popular sites like Facebook where you can easily navigate for specified groups and events to take part in. Other popular places to find support can
be local kid-focused facilities like park districts, daycares, school events, and even playgrounds. These locations are all great options to meet people
who have a mutual focus on finding a balance and a bigger support system to accomplish that goal. Many credited health sites like the Country Health Ranking,
encourage group-based parenting programs for it's benefits in mental health amongst parents and their children.
